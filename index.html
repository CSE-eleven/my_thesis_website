Introduction

OSCC is one of the leading causes of cancer-related deaths. The biggest challenge is late diagnosis, which leads to high mortality rates.

To solve this, early detection is crucial, but current methods lack:

Accessibility

Expertise

Explainability

This research aims to combine deep learning (DL) with explainable AI (XAI) to develop a model that improves diagnostic accuracy and makes early screening accessible to more people.

Objective

To develop a deep learning-based framework for detecting and classifying oral lesions from medical images

To integrate Explainable AI (XAI) techniques for decision-making by comparing new cases with similar historical cases, ensuring transparency and interpretability.

To evaluate the performance of the system using metrics such as accuracy, detection rate, and classification rate.

Literature Review

Explainable screening of oral cancer via deep learning and case-based reasoning Mario G.C.A., Cimino, Giuseppina, Campisi, et al.

Publisher: Elsevier Year: 2025, Pages: Smart Health 35 (2025) 100538, Organization: University of Pisa, University of Palermo, DOI: 10.1016/j.smhl.2024.100538


Dataset: online source, Algorithm/Model: Faster R-CNN + Feature Pyramid Networks (FPN), XAI Model: CBR, Result : 83% detection accuracy, 92% classification accuracy, Limitation: Requires periodic updates and retraining, dataset size is limited.

Towards explainable oral cancer recognition: Screening on imperfect images via Informed Deep Learning and Case-Based Reasoning Marco Parola,Federico A.Galatolo, et al.

Publisher: Elsevier, Year: 2024, Pages: Computerized Medical Imaging and Graphics 117 (2024) 102433, Organization: University of Pisa, University of Palermo, DOI: 10.1016/j.compmedimag.2024.102433


Dataset:kaggle, Algorithm/Model: Informed Deep Learning (IDL) + Ensemble DL, XAI Model: CBR, Result : 85% accuracy, Limitation: Data imperfections (artifacts, labeling errors), requires expert validation.

An Explainable Deep Learning Approach for Oral Cancer Detection P. Ashok Babu, Anjani Kumar Rai, et al.

Publisher: Springer, Year: 2024, Pages: Journal of Electrical Engineering & Technology 19:1837–1848, Organization: Institute of Aeronautical Engineering, GLA University, DOI: 10.1007/s42835-023-01654-1


Dataset: Custom dataset of oral cancer images, Algorithm/Model: Inception-V3, XAI Model: FCM, Feature Selection,Performance Evaluation Metrics, Result : F1-Score = 86.9, Sensitivity = 79.7, Precision = 92.9, Limitation: It may be possible to improve the accuracy with all classes.

Hybrid Deep Learning Model for the Detection of Oral Cancer Lee et al

Publisher: IEEE, Year: 2024, Pages: Volume 12, Pages 1234-1245, Organization: Seoul National University, South Korea, DOI: 10.1016/j.identj.2022.03.001


Dataset: Oral cancer images from clinical settings, Algorithm/Model: CNN + RNN, Result: AUC: 0.97, Sensitivity: 95%, Specificity: 94%, Limitation: The model requires significant preprocessing of images; complexity may hinder real-time clinical application.
